  query = Describe the geology of the Grand Canyon
  answer = The Grand Canyon region is underlain by a vast pile of gently northward-dipping, stratified rocks more than 10,000 ft thick. 
           These beds form a broad platform from which rises a flight of immense “stairs” or terraces, each capped by a resistant layer that protects weaker strata beneath.
           The retreating edges of these caprocks leave sheer escarpments, mesas, and buttes whose cliffs are carved into cathedral-like walls, pyramids, towers, arches, and colonnades. 
           Weathering and wind deflation steadily strip away the softer layers, so the plateau is gradually dissected and lowered toward a bare-rock or gravel-strewn plain.

  query = Describe the geology of Petrified National forest
  answer = The Petrified Forest region preserves a record of Late Triassic lowlands that once extended westward from the rising Appalachian mountain system.  
           At that time the area lay near the equator and was crossed by slow, meandering rivers that deposited great thicknesses of reddish sand and silt.  
           Periodic floods buried vast forests of conifers, cycad-like plants, and giant horsetails (similar to the forty-foot calamites described for the Carboniferous).  
           The buried trunks were permeated by silica-rich groundwater, which gradually replaced the organic tissue with quartz, creating the brilliantly colored, perfectly preserved petrified logs visible today.  
           Above these fossil-bearing layers lie younger Triassic strata that record continued river and flood-plain conditions; the entire sequence rests unconformably on the eroded edges of the older, folded Paleozoic rocks that had been uplifted during the Appalachian deformation.
